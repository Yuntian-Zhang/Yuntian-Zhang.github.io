Short Bio

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Automation and also with the National Key Lab of Autonomous Intelligent Unmanned Systems, Beijing Institute of Technology (BIT), Beijing, China. I am a Visiting Ph.D. Student in the Department of Computer, Control and Management Engineering “Antonio Ruberti” (DIAG) at Sapienza University of Rome, Roma, Italy from 2025 to 2026. I received the B.Eng. degree with honors in Automation from BIT in 2021. I was a Visiting Student in the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at University of Washington, Seattle, USA, in 2019.

Research

✨ In the broadest sense, I do research regarding how to bridge Operations Research (OR) and Machine Learning (ML) together for solving complex Real-World Systems under Uncertainty. My expertise lies in

  • Reformulation and Decomposition of Integer Programming
  • Prescriptive Analytics with Limited Data
  • Real-World Applications in Location and Covering, Spatial Analysis and Optimization, Routing, etc.

    • I have more than 10 papers published or in progress in principal Journals such as INFORMS Journal on Computing (UTD 24), IEEE/ACM Transactions on Networking (CCF A), IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems and Conferences including IEEE CDC, ACM SIGSPATIAL, ACM SIGEVO GECCO. I have also applied or been issued more than 10 national invention patents in China or USA.

    🌈 With my collaborators, I am currently engaged in OR4ML, OR4Science on Next-generation computing systems.
